Chaos Theory

October 2024

Chaos is not randomness. It is a break in the link between cause and effect. When effect cannot be tidily traced back to a singular cause, predicting the future becomes challenging. Assuming rational economic agents driving human nature, and abstracting from unpredictable and irrational behaviour has left gaping areas for research.

A growing body of research suggests that economic, political, and psychological systems are governed by nonlinear dynamics of complex systems.

In this course, we will dive into a successful problem-solving approach for chaotic systems - the theories of nonlinear complexity systems. These theories have enabled natural scientists to make leaps forward in quantum physics, computational biology, and meteorology.

Theories of Democracy

October 2024

Democracy is an extremely saturated word. Different people meant different things. Democracy in ancient Athens was completely different than today yet we use the same word to refer to both these things. Conservatives, leftists, liberals, anarchists, communists; they all talk about democracy yet they usually mean different things. Join us in a deep yet short dive into all the different theories of democracy and how each one compares to the rest.
