Let’s Learn Together

Shoshin College is an experimental learning co-operative operation that’s positioning itself outside of the existing way of how learning and teaching works.

We aim to universal. We embrace all domains and disciplines and invite everyone to teach and learn, regardless of background.

We aim to be inclusive. We believe in high quality work but we also believe that anyone can do high quality work. We do not exclude people because of the quality of their work. We include them as we want them to improve their work.

We aim to be in-person. There is tons of online learning material. What’s missing is humane interactions in a community of people.

We aim to be ever-fluctuating. We embrace iteration and invite impermanence. Experimentation and iteration.

We aim to be unaccredited and non-degree-granting. Accredited institutions are great, they are doing their thing. We are trying something new and in order to explore it, we need not be tied down by the existing ways of how learning works today.

We aim to be high quality. We could succeed in starting a learning institution but what’s interesting is to make it high quality and more importantly: higher quality than the current way of learning institutions.

We aim to be low-cost. Wealth inequality contributes to a decreasing average quality of life for the Western resident. We are not interested in adding to this.
